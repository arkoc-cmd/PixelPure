Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about RAW photo conversions on PixelPure.

A RAW file contains minimally processed data from the image sensor of a digital camera. RAW files are uncompressed and capture the maximum amount of detail, dynamic range, and color information possible, which gives photographers total control during editing but results in large file sizes that need conversion to be easily shared.

PixelPure supports all major camera brands and formats including Canon (CR2, CR3), Nikon (NEF), Sony (ARW), Fujifilm (RAF), Olympus (ORF), Panasonic (RW2), Pentax (PEF), and the universal Adobe Digital Negative (DNG) standard.

Yes, batch conversion is fully supported. You can select and upload up to 100 RAW files at once. The dashboard allows you to configure conversion settings uniformly across the entire batch or individually.

Yes. By default, PixelPure retains all core EXIF, IPTC, and XMP camera details including ISO speed, exposure time, aperture details, lens profiles, capture date, and copyright credentials. You can choose to strip this metadata in the settings sidebar if you prefer privacy.

You can convert your RAW images into standard JPEG, lossless PNG, next-gen high-efficiency WEBP, or high-fidelity professional TIFF output files. You can choose your output format in the dashboard sidebar settings.

PixelPure features client-side browser-based execution. Files are processed entirely locally on your processor via WebAssembly. Your photos never touch our servers, meaning there is zero threat of exposure or unpermitted data collection.

Absolutely. Once the conversion progress finishes, you can download individual files directly or compile the entire batch of converted images into a compressed ZIP file with a single click.
